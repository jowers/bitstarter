Fantasy Football

Just like in standard fantasy football competitions, you pick a squad of players, and each week pick your team from that squad. Your players earn you points for goals, assists and clean sheets, and lose you points for conceding goals and getting sent off.

However, unlike the standard games, these points are converted directly into pounds and can be spent on improving your squad. Come the end of the season, it's the team that has the highest total value - that is total of players and points - that wins.

Fantasy Trading

Rather than just having a player for each position, you buy shares in players. So, in goal, you could have one share in Petr Cech. However, you could instead have 5 shares in Julian Speroni.

The value of each player will increase and decrease based on market value - just like the stock market. So, after a few weeks, your 5 shares in Julian Speroni could now be worth 2 Petr Cechs. Of course, it could also go the other way.

As each week goes on, re-invest your money and choose when to buy more shares in your existing players, and when to trade in for a better model.

FAQ

How can I stay updated about the Footdaq project?
You can follow us on twitter or for more detail, the Footdaq blog will provide updates on the development process. Questions by email are also welcomed and we will endeavor to respond either directly or via the blog.
Will there be a mobile app available for the game?
Not for the initial launch. The website will be html5 and be designed responsively, so mobile users will be able to take part without requiring an app.
What competitions will this cover?

The first season will cover the English Premier League (EPL). Future seasons will expand to offer some of the other top leagues.

Are there any plans for any other sports?
We are concentrating on football for the first version. If that is successful, and there is demand for other sports, we will certainly be looking at transferring the format across.
When will the first competition start?
The game will be ready for the start of the 2014-15 season. Later in the 2013-14 season, we'll be running some beta tests so that we are ready to go next summer.
